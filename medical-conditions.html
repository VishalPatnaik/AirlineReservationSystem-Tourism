Medical Conditions

Heart conditions

Coronary Thrombosis (The most frequent cause of so called heart attack) patients may travel if a normal recovery has been made. In the usual case, patients should not fly before six weeks from date of attack. The doctor often advises these patients not to climb stairs, but can walk on the level.

Sitting upright 

For the safety of all our guests, every guest must be able to sit upright during take-off and landing.  Some guests with a disability may need assistance to sit upright; an Orthotic Positioning Device (OPD) or brace may be used on the aircraft to meet this requirement.  If a guest is not able to sit upright during taxi, take-off and landing, they will need to bring a device that assists them to sit upright such as an Orthotic Positioning Device.  This device cannot be attached to the seat back and the seat belt needs to be the method by which the guest is secured in the seat.  Any type of OPD or brace is acceptable if is not attached directly to the aircraft seat and supports the guest so they can sit upright.  In addition, the OPD or brace must fit between the armrests in the down position and not infringe on another guest's seat.  Guests using an OPD may not sit in an emergency row.  Vol En Avion does not supply guests with OPDs.  

POC (Portable Oxygen Concentrator)

Oxygen (O2) is classified under the category of restricted articles, as a compressed gas of any kind is hazardous material.  Persons who might require occasional or constant oxygen may wish to consider other air carrier alternatives. Vol En Avion cannot allow guests who require oxygen nor will Vol En Avion provide oxygen for guests use other than in emergency situations.

Vol En Avion does however accept certain carry-on respiratory assistive devices that assist with an individual’s respiratory functions including ventilators, respirators and continuous positive airway pressure machines (CPAPs) cleared by Vol En Avion, and the following FAA approved portable oxygen concentrators (POCs):

  • SeQual Oxywell Oxygen System (model 4000)
  • SeQual SAROS
  • VBOX Trooper Oxygen Concentrator

Guests must inform Vol En Avion their intent to use the Respiratory Assistive Device in-flight a minimum of forty-eight (48) hours prior to their flight.  Our Care Team Desk will ensure the device you are planning to use in-flight is an approved device.

If you will be using a POC in-flight, you will also be required to present a physician’s statement with the following information:

  • Must have a physician’s signature
  • Notation which phases of flight (taxi, takeoff, cruise, landing etc.) oxygen use is medically necessary
  • The physician’s statement must be updated whenever your health changes in such a way that affects your fitness to fly with supplemental oxygen.Guests are responsible for bringing an adequate number of fully charged batteries to power the respiratory assistive device for no less than 150% of the maximum flight duration as unfortunately, sources suggest that aircraft electrical outlets may not be reliable sources of power for electronic respiratory assistive devices, and thus out of concern for our guests we do not permit guests to use power outlets to use or charge these devices.

If you would like to fly on Vol En Avion with a respiratory assistive device, please print the Respiratory Device Medical Authorization document. It can either be used by your physician to provide the required statement, or provide guidance as to what should be included in such a statement.

To contact a member of the Vol En Avion Care Team and obtain more information about Respiratory Devices or submit your medical authorization please contact them here.

On board oxygen is not provided upon request. If a guest will need oxygen they are required to bring a respiratory assistive device with them. Please note: Vol En Avion does not allow oxygen tanks or canisters as checked or carry-on baggage. 

Intravenous medication

Guests with intravenous devices and medications may use certain supplies onboard the aircraft. These include IV supplies, like medications, needles, and syringes. If you have any further questions, please visit the TSA for detailed information about disposal methods, proper containment, labeling information, and more.
