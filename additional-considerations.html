Additional Considerations

Guests requiring assistants

The following guests will need a safety assistant:

  • Any Guest, because of a mental disability, cannot understand or respond appropriately to safety instructions from Vol En Avion teammates.
  • Any Guest with a mobility impairment so severe that he or she would not be able to physically assist in his or her evacuation from the aircraft during an emergency.
  • Any Guest who has both severe hearing and vision impairments, if the guest cannot communicate with Vol En Avion teammates and allow them to give the safety requirements.

Guests who advise Vol En Avion that they fit into one of the above categories will be required to provide their own safety assistant. The safety assistant must pay the fare applicable at the time of making a reservation.

Guests not requiring assistants

Vol En Avion will not require a safety assistant if all of the following conditions can be met:

  • The Guest has a method of communication sufficient to receive a safety briefing.
  • The Guest is able to physically assist in his or her own evacuation of the aircraft in the event of an emergency.
  • The Guest is able to comprehend and, if necessary, respond to safety instructions from Vol En Avion Team Members.

Vol En Avion may, however, override a guest’s self-assessment and require a guest to travel with a safety assistant under certain circumstances.   If Vol En Avion determines that a Guest requires an assistant, but the Guest believes he or she is capable of traveling without an assistant, Vol En Avion may require an assistant - either a Vol En Avion team member or other assistant selected by Vol En Avion, or an assistant selected by the Guest.  In this situation, Vol En Avion will not charge for the transportation of the Guest's assistant.

Deaf or Hard of Hearing: 

Guests who are deaf or hard of hearing can use the free 711 service provided by the federal government.

If you’re travelling alone, upon request, Vol En Avion is happy to assist you with moving through the airport and pre-boarding the aircraft if necessary.

Blind or Low Vision: 

If you’re travelling alone, upon request, Vol En Avion is happy to assist you with moving through the airport and pre-boarding the aircraft if necessary.

Other Accommodations

We offer a variety of services to assist guests with disabilities.  Our aircraft are equipped with features for accommodating guests who need assistance, including accessible lavatories.

In order to provide you with the assistance you need, we may need up to 24 hours advance notice and one-hour advance check in for guests with disabilities who request the following service(s):

  • Accommodating 10 or more individuals with disabilities who are traveling as a group
  • Wet cell battery box for a personal wheelchair

We have special procedures for wheelchair stowage.  If a wheelchair requires disassembly, guests should provide written directions for disassembling and reassembling the wheelchair.
