Service Animals

A service animal is permitted to accompany a guest with a disability on Vol En Avion. As proof of the animal's status, the guest can present:

  • An identification card for the animal
  • Presence of harness or markings on harness tag
  • The guest's credible verbal statement

For travel to Hawaii a health certificate dated maximum 14 days prior to travel is required. A health certificate is valid for 30 days from the original departure date. If return date is outside of 30 days, a new health certificate is required.

Your service animal must occupy your own space and cannot be in a seat. Also, they cannot obstruct aisles or areas that must remain clear for emergency evacuation to comply with FAA regulations. Service animals are not required to be in a pet carrier. If a service animal meets these criteria, then it can be carried with no charge to the guest.

Guests may not travel with service animals that pose unavoidable safety and/or public health concerns. Snakes, other reptiles, rodents, and spiders fall within this category of animals.

Emotional Support Animals

Emotional support animals may accompany a Guest with a disability on a flight. Vol En Avion requires documentation to substantiate the Guest's disability-related need for the animal to accompany them in the aircraft cabin.

Acceptable forms of documentation include a letter from a licensed mental health professional (e.g. psychiatrist, psychologist, licensed clinical social worker). The letter may not be more than one year old from the date of the guest's scheduled initial flight. It must include verification of all of the following information:

  • That the guest has a mental health-related disability; and
  • That having the animal accompany the guest is necessary for the guest's mental health, treatment, or to assist the guest (with his or her disability); and
  • That the individual providing the assessment of the guest is a licensed mental health professional; and
  • That the guest is under the care of the individual providing the documentation; and
  • Health certificate dated maximum 14 days prior to travel (Hawaii only)

For travel to Hawaii a health certificate dated maximum 14 days prior to travel is required. A health certificate is valid for 30 days from the original departure date. If return date is outside of 30 days, a new health certificate is required.

International Travel

For animals traveling between the United States and Mexico, there are additional restrictions, please see our FAQ on International Pet Travel.

Interline Travel

If you are traveling on an Interline partner, please contact their reservations to find out their policies for Service and Emotional Support Animals.

Traveling to Hawaii

To arrange Service Animal entry into Hawaii, it is the responsibility of the customer to contact the Hawaii Department of Agriculture, Animal Quarantine Station for all specific information.

Guests are responsible to obtain health certificates for their pets prior to the first departure date as follows:

From HA to North America No certificate needed
From North America to Hawaii 14 days prior to travel
